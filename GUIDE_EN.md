# 🛁🐱 GitHub Octocat Bath Bomb — Complete Crafting Guide

A black, lavender-scented bath bomb shaped like the GitHub Octocat.
The **two-piece clamshell mold** holds the shape firmly while it cures, then
opens like a shell so the bath bomb pops out cleanly.

## 🔧 Phase A: 3D Printing

### Slicer Settings (Cura, etc.)
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Files | `mold_cat_clamshell_A.stl` + `mold_cat_clamshell_B.stl` |
| Filament | PLA |
| Layer height | 0.2mm |
| Infill | 20–30% |
| Wall thickness | 1.2mm (3 walls) |
| Support | **None** |
| Brim | **Yes** (prevents warping) |
| Nozzle temp | 200–210°C |
| Bed temp | 60°C |

💡 **Print orientation tip**: Print each half with the **flange face up** (cavity-back side on the bed). This keeps the pin/hole geometry crisp and the flange flat for a tight seal.

### After Printing
1. Remove brim with a knife
2. Sand the cavity interior:
   - **#400 grit** → remove layer lines
   - **#800 grit** → smooth finish
3. Lightly sand the **flange face** with #400 to ensure it's perfectly flat (better seal)
4. Test-fit the two halves: pegs should slide into holes with a snug press fit

## 🧪 Phase D: Making the Bath Bomb

### D-1. Mix Dry Ingredients
```
Add to bowl in order:
  ① Baking soda       200g
  ② Citric acid        100g
  ③ Cornstarch          50g
  ④ Charcoal powder     1–2 tsp (start small!)
```
- Wear rubber gloves! (charcoal stains are hard to remove)
- Mix thoroughly with a fork, breaking up any clumps (2–3 min)

### D-2. Add Wet Ingredients
```
  ⑤ Coconut oil        1–2 tbsp (melt in microwave if solid)
  ⑥ Lavender EO        10–15 drops
```
- Mix well

### D-3. Adjust Moisture
```
  ⑦ Pour rubbing alcohol into spray bottle
    → Spritz 1–2 pumps at a time while mixing
```
- 🎯 **Target consistency**: Holds shape when squeezed, crumbles when lightly pressed
  - Like "wet sand" texture
  - Too crumbly → one more spritz
  - Too wet → add a little baking soda

- ⚠️ **NEVER use water!** It triggers the fizzing reaction

### D-4. Fill the Clamshell Mold

```
   Half A (pin side)         Half B (hole side)
   ┌─────────────────┐       ┌─────────────────┐
   │  ① Coat cavity   │       │  ① Coat cavity   │   ← thin smear of oil
   │     with oil     │       │     with oil     │     (or line with wrap)
   │                  │       │                  │
   │  ② Heap mixture  │       │  ② Heap mixture  │   ← slightly above
   │     in cavity ↑↑↑│       │     in cavity ↑↑↑│     the cavity rim
   │                  │       │                  │
   │  ③ Pack firmly   │       │  ③ Pack firmly   │
   └─────────────────┘       └─────────────────┘
                  ↓ clamp ↓
            ┌─────────────────┐
            │ Press the two    │  ← align pins into holes
            │ halves together  │     and push straight down
            │ (the heaped mix  │
            │  compresses for  │
            │  a tight bond)   │
            └─────────────────┘
                  ↓ secure ↓
            ┌─────────────────┐
            │  ④ Wrap rubber   │  ← cross-pattern (X) over
            │     bands tightly│     the flange
            └─────────────────┘
```

#### Detailed Steps:
1. **Coat the cavity**: Smear a thin layer of cooking oil (or coconut oil) on the interior of each half with a finger or paper towel. (Better than plastic wrap for a clamshell — wrap tends to wrinkle in this geometry.)
2. **Fill both halves**: Divide the mixture roughly in half and **heap it slightly above the rim** of each cavity (the excess will compress when you close the clamshell).
3. **Pack firmly**: Press the mixture in each half with your fingertips.
4. **Close the clamshell**: Place half B (holes) onto half A (pins), aligning the pegs into the holes. Press straight down firmly until the flanges meet.
5. **Clamp with rubber bands**: Wrap rubber bands across the flange in an X pattern (one vertical, one horizontal). They must be tight — slack means a gap and a visible seam.

---

## ⏳ Phase E: Drying

1. Leave the clamped clamshell on parchment paper
2. Place in a **well-ventilated area** for **24–48 hours**
3. ⚠️ **Don't touch it!** It's extremely fragile while drying
4. ⚠️ Avoid humid areas (bathroom, kitchen)
5. Summer: 24 hours. Winter: 48 hours.

---

## 🎁 Phase F: Finishing & Wrapping

### Opening the Clamshell
1. Carefully remove the rubber bands
2. Hold the flange with both hands and **gently pull the two halves apart** sideways (like opening a clam)
3. The bath bomb usually stays in one half — flip that half over and **let it drop into your palm**
4. If it sticks, **tap the back of the half lightly** with your other hand
5. ⚠️ Be gentle — ears and tentacle are fragile

### Touch-up
- A faint flash line may appear along the parting plane — smooth it with a finger
- If a corner chipped, press leftover mixture onto it to repair

### Gift Wrapping
1. Wrap the bath bomb tightly in plastic wrap (moisture barrier)
2. Place in a clear gift bag with a ribbon
3. Include a usage card (see below)

## ⚠️ Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Won't hold shape / crumbly | Not enough alcohol | Add 1–2 more spritzes |
| Started fizzing during mixing | Water got in | Mold quickly. Next time use only alcohol |
| Won't release from cavity | Forgot release agent / packed too tight | Always coat with oil. Try 30 min in freezer |
| Clamshell won't close | Too much mixture in the halves | Heap less — just enough to compress on close |
| Visible seam / gap at parting line | Rubber bands too loose | Use stronger bands in a tight X pattern |
| Ears broke off | Thin sections are fragile | Repair with leftover mixture. Pack thicker next time |
| Cracked while drying | Dried too fast / uneven thickness | Move to a less drafty spot; the closed mold dries it slowly which is good |
| Water turned pitch black | Too much charcoal | Use ½ tsp next time |
| Bathtub stained | Charcoal / food coloring | Clean with melamine sponge. Charcoal stains less |

## 💡 Tips for Success

1. **Practice first**: Make a small round bath bomb by hand to get the feel
2. **Charcoal — less is more**: ½ tsp is enough for deep black. Too much = pitch-black bath water
3. **Release agent — go thin**: Too much oil makes the bath bomb surface greasy
4. **Pack firmly**: Harder than you think. Weak packing = crumbling
5. **Alcohol — go slow**: Too little is better than too much
6. **Store sealed**: Keep finished bath bomb away from moisture. Gift within 1–2 weeks
