# 🛁🐱 GitHub Octocat Bath Bomb — Complete Crafting Guide

A black, lavender-scented bath bomb shaped like the GitHub Octocat.
A waterproofed photo (9cm × 5.5cm) is hidden inside — revealed as a surprise when it dissolves!

## 🔧 Phase A: 3D Printing

### Slicer Settings (Cura, etc.)
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| File | `mold_cat_container.stl` |
| Filament | PLA |
| Layer height | 0.2mm |
| Infill | 20–30% |
| Wall thickness | 1.2mm (3 walls) |
| Support | **None** |
| Brim | **Yes** (prevents warping) |
| Nozzle temp | 200–210°C |
| Bed temp | 60°C |

### After Printing
1. Remove brim with a knife
2. Sand the cavity interior:
   - **#400 grit** → remove layer lines
   - **#800 grit** → smooth finish
3. Run your finger inside — it should feel smooth

---

## 📷 Phase D: Waterproofing the Photo

### Steps
1. Print your photo at **85mm × 55mm or smaller** (photo booth or home printer)
   - ⚠️ The mold cavity is 86×92mm. Orient the photo's long side along the 92mm axis
   - ⚠️ Inkjet prints are NOT waterproof — lamination is **mandatory**
2. Apply self-adhesive laminating film on both sides
   - Leave **5mm+ margin** around the photo for a tight seal
   - Press edges firmly with a fingernail or ruler
3. Trim excess film around the photo

### ⚠️ Waterproof Test (Required!)
- Submerge the laminated photo in water for **5 minutes**
- Remove, dry, and check if the photo got wet
- If wet → redo the lamination

## 🧪 Phase E: Making the Bath Bomb

### E-1. Mix Dry Ingredients
```
Add to bowl in order:
  ① Baking soda       200g
  ② Citric acid        100g
  ③ Cornstarch          50g
  ④ Charcoal powder     1–2 tsp (start small!)
```
- Wear rubber gloves! (charcoal stains are hard to remove)
- Mix thoroughly with a fork, breaking up any clumps (2–3 min)

### E-2. Add Wet Ingredients
```
  ⑤ Coconut oil        1–2 tbsp (melt in microwave if solid)
  ⑥ Lavender EO        10–15 drops
```
- Mix well

### E-3. Adjust Moisture
```
  ⑦ Pour rubbing alcohol into spray bottle
    → Spritz 1–2 pumps at a time while mixing
```
- 🎯 **Target consistency**: Holds shape when squeezed, crumbles when lightly pressed
  - Like "wet sand" texture
  - Too crumbly → one more spritz
  - Too wet → add a little baking soda

- ⚠️ **NEVER use water!** It triggers the fizzing reaction

### E-4. Fill the Mold

```
    ┌── Cat-shaped mold ──┐
    │                      │
    │  ① Line with wrap    │  ← Press wrap into walls and floor
    │                      │
    │  ② Fill halfway      │  ← Pack firmly with fingers
    │     ↓↓↓↓↓↓          │
    │  ③ Place photo 📷    │  ← Center it flat
    │                      │
    │  ④ Fill to the top   │  ← Pack firmly
    │     ↓↓↓↓↓↓          │
    │  ⑤ Flatten with      │  ← Scrape level with spatula
    │     spatula           │
    └──────────────────────┘
```

#### Detailed Steps:
1. **Line with wrap**: Press plastic wrap into the mold cavity
   - Minimize wrinkles (they'll transfer to the bath bomb)
   - Leave the wrap edges hanging over the sides (for lifting later)
2. **Fill halfway**: Add mixture to half depth, press firmly with fingers
3. **Place photo**: Lay the laminated photo flat in the center
   - Long side (85mm) along the mold's 92mm axis
4. **Fill to top**: Add remaining mixture, press firmly
   - Make sure the photo is fully covered
5. **Flatten**: Scrape the top level with a spatula or ruler

## ⏳ Phase F: Drying

1. Leave in the mold on parchment paper
2. Place in a **well-ventilated area** for **24–48 hours**
3. ⚠️ **Don't touch it!** It's extremely fragile while drying
4. ⚠️ Avoid humid areas (bathroom, kitchen)
5. Summer: 24 hours. Winter: 48 hours.

---

## 🎁 Phase G: Finishing & Wrapping

### Demolding
1. Flip the mold upside down
2. Gently pull the plastic wrap edges to lift the bath bomb out
3. Carefully peel the wrap off
4. ⚠️ Be gentle — ears and tentacle are fragile!

### Touch-up
- Smooth any wrap wrinkle marks with your finger
- If a corner chipped, press leftover mixture onto it to repair

### Gift Wrapping
1. Wrap the bath bomb tightly in plastic wrap (moisture barrier)
2. Place in a clear gift bag with a ribbon
3. Include a usage card (see below)

## ⚠️ Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Won't hold shape / crumbly | Not enough alcohol | Add 1–2 more spritzes |
| Started fizzing during mixing | Water got in | Mold quickly. Next time use only alcohol |
| Won't come out of mold | Forgot plastic wrap | Always use wrap. Try 30 min in freezer |
| Ears broke off | Thin sections are fragile | Repair with leftover mixture. Pack thicker next time |
| Cracked while drying | Dried too fast / uneven thickness | Move to less drafty spot. Cover loosely with wrap |
| Photo got wet | Lamination leak | Re-press edges or redo lamination |
| Water turned pitch black | Too much charcoal | Use ½ tsp next time |
| Bathtub stained | Charcoal / food coloring | Clean with melamine sponge. Charcoal stains less |

---

## 💡 Tips for Success

1. **Practice first**: Make a small round bath bomb by hand to get the feel
2. **Charcoal — less is more**: ½ tsp is enough for deep black. Too much = pitch-black bath water
3. **Plastic wrap is key**: Ensures clean release and hygiene
4. **Pack firmly**: Harder than you think. Weak packing = crumbling
5. **Alcohol — go slow**: Too little is better than too much
6. **Store sealed**: Keep finished bath bomb away from moisture. Gift within 1–2 weeks
